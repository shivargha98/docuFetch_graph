# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert sunlight into chemical
energy stored in glucose. It takes place inside the chloroplast, an organelle
found in plant cells.

The process depends on chlorophyll, the green pigment that absorbs sunlight.
Without chlorophyll, a plant cannot capture light energy efficiently.

Photosynthesis produces oxygen as a byproduct, which is released into the
atmosphere and used by cellular respiration in animals.
